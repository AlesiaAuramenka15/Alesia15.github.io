Endless legends surround the mystery of "La Joconde ". Art historians, journalists and enthusiasts have been arguing for decades about what Mona Lisa's smile means, is it not a fake that hangs in the Louvre and who is depicted in the Leonardo’s masterpiece? How did it happen that it was “Gioconda” who won the “world championship” among all the works and “art” selfies?

Try to do the impossible and forget that “Gioconda” is a picture of pictures. What do you see in front of you? A small-sized portrait of a not very beautiful and modestly dressed woman. Why did she press such strong rivals like Nefertiti, Raphael's Sistine Madonna, or Venus in front of a mirror by Velasquez on the podium of honor? To answer this question, it is not enough to know the milestones of the Mona Lisa's victorious path to world fame. It is much more important to understand not “how it was,” but “why it happened.” That is to understand this mechanism.

Most people are sure that the portrait of Leonardo is Mona Lisa, wife of Francesco del Giocondo.Hence the second name of the painting - “Gioconda”. The real Mona Lisa got married at sixteen for a widower who is much older than her, who had buried two wives by that time. She lived the mundane of a noblewoman married just for money to someone her parents had chosen.

Another contribution to the romantic aura that surrounds “Gioconda” is a play about a touching love story that happened between the genius Leonardo and his model. Its author is the great visionary Jules Verne.In his early play "The Mona Lisa" he portrayed her as a mistress of the great Florentine. Thus, in the minds of reading Europe, a love triangle arose- a young handsome artist, an old merchant husband, and a beautiful Gioconda. And no one cared that in reality Mona Lisa’s husband was much younger than Leonardo, and the artist himself was persecuted for homosexuality.

The mystery of her enigmatic smile is second to none, but it is also so widely discussed that it feels that another riddle is completely ruled out. In order to do that masterpiece justice justice, another question that has long fascinated art experts will be mentioned: what happened to Mona Lisa's eyebrows? "If you look closely at Mona Lisa's eye you can clearly see that the cracks around the eye have slightly disappeared, and that may be explained that one day a curator or restorer cleaned the eye, and cleaning the eye removed, probably removed the eyelashes and eyebrow,"

On August 21, 1911, an artist came to the Square Salon of the Louvre, who decided to write a copy of Mona Lisa. To his surprise, where the painting usually hung, there was a gaping void. “Probably visiting the photographers from the weekend, they will bring back soon,” his servants reassured. Time passed - the picture did not appear. The artist began panicing, tryong to draw everyone's attention to the mysterious gap in the display, and the ministers went to hurry the photographers.They looked confused and answered that they did not have any intyention of photograping her. It downed on everyone that "Mona Lisa" was stolen.

The security finally realized that the picture was stolen. A terrible scandal broke out. The director of Louvre Omol, who only recently boasted that stealing a “Gioconda” is like kidnapping Notre Dame, was fired. Unexpectedly all of a sudden “Mona Lisa” was in the center of political battles. France longed for revenge, desired the defeat of Germany that was their enemy since 1870. A roaring European war broke out.

The main character in the case of the theft of "Mona Lisa" was not the police, but the press, and she generously repaid the portrait by Leonardo for the opportunity to demonstrate her growing power. Theft of the picture was the first truly world-wide sensation. The press appointed and dismissed the directors of the Louvre and the prefects of the police, decided whether to be a European war or not, and most importantly - fascinated everyone, from aristocrat to commoner, with endless detective story options: “Who stole Mona Lisa?”

One of the main suspects was avant-garde leader Pablo Picasso. It turned out that his friend, a certain Pierre, had stolen two ancient stone figurines from the heart of the world of the antiques. Picasso considered primitive artists to be the forerunners of the Cubists. He always wanted to have their works besides him all the time. Museums, he considered the tombs of art, where it is hidden from real life. he police decided that, having stolen the figurines, the avant-gardists got a taste and decided to get their hands on Leonardo's masterpiece. The detectives suspected Guillaume Apollinaire, who was the “leader” of the international gang of avant-garde thieves, they carried out an investigation. The poet ended up being the only person arrested in the Mona Lisa case. To the credit of the police, it must be said that the innocence of Apollinaire was established almost immediately. avant-garde was announced innocent.

The only evidence found in this case belonged to Alphonse Bertillon who at that time was a French policeman. During the course of the investigation he noticed a fingerprint on the frame. Despite such a striking a crucial piece of evidence, he simply neglected it as the father of the world's first system for identifying criminals based on examining fingerprints was his main opponent and enemy. Bertillon did not have the foggiest idea how to use this amaxing finding, consequently it led to the clue that could solve one of the prime riddles of the art world becoming of no use. Detectives, driven by a pressing desire of getting famous and loved by publicity, got out of their way in order to achieve at least something, which resulted in failure and bad reputation.

Only the death of the Titanic could compete with the mystery of Mona Lisa. And on December 2, 1913 - after more than two years after the painting disappered - the unknown, who identified himself as Leonard (who was actually called Vincenzo Perugia), offered Florentine antiquarian Alfredo Gehry to buy “Gioconda”. The stranger explained that his goal was to return to Italy a masterpiece, stolen by Napoleon. Leonard simply did not know that France had bought the painting three hundred years before Napoleon came to power. After some time, he brought a picture from Paris to Florence in a road box with a double bottom. There, littered with dirty shirts and socks, It was revieled to the police, after he was reported, which led to the end of the mystery. Leonard was arrested. What he said during interrogations caused a new scandal.The press played the grand performance: “The Return of“ Mona Lisa ”. For the second time in three years, the picture turned out to be the heroine of a world sensation. Perugia was hiding the "Gioconda" under the mattress. So he slept in the picture for more than two years.

“Mona Lisa” was shown at exhibitions in Florence, Rome and Milan, and then she returned to France in triumph in a separate compartment of the Milan-Paris express train. Perugia received only a year in prison - Italian court took into account his patriotic motives. During the First World War, he fought bravely, returned as a hero, and lived the life of a small shopkeeper - a painter. After the theft and return of the painting finally became a symbol of the visual arts in general. The picture turned into a true celebrity, its name is synonymous to a masterpiece, its smile is a symbol of mystery.
